Indian Firms Test Regional-Language AI Customer Support Tools Bengaluru, July 3: A group of Indian technology companies has started testing AI-powered customer support systems that can understand regional languages and respond to users in a more natural conversational style. The systems are being piloted in Tamil, Hindi, Telugu, Kannada and Bengali, with the aim of helping banks, e-commerce platforms and public service providers handle large volumes of user queries. Companies involved in the trials said the tools are designed to support human agents rather than replace them fully. According to project teams, the AI systems can identify the intent of a customer query, retrieve relevant information from approved company documents and draft a response in the user’s preferred language. In sensitive cases such as payment disputes, account access issues or legal complaints, the query is routed to a human support executive. Industry experts said regional-language AI could improve access for users who are not comfortable with English. However, they warned that such systems must be carefully tested to avoid incorrect responses, privacy risks and over-reliance on automation. Some companies are also adding audit trails so every AI-generated response can be reviewed later. This is expected to help compliance teams verify whether the system followed approved policies. The pilot projects are expected to continue for three to six months before wider rollout decisions are taken.
